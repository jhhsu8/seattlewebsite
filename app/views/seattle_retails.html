Pike Place Market

Pike Place Market is a public market located next to the Elliott Bay waterfront (part of Puget Sound) in Downtown Seattle. The Market, opened on August 17, 1907, is one of the oldest public farmers' markets in the country. It is Seattle's most popular tourist destination and is the 33rd most visited tourist destination in the world. It has more than 10 million visitors each year.

The Market is a business place for fishmongers, farmers, craftspeople and merchants to sell their products. It consists of a main level and several lower levels located below the main level. The main level has a wide variety of seafood, fresh produces, fresh flowers and crafts on stands. The lower levels has small shops such as antique shops, comic book shops, collectible shops, and historical head shops.

Din Tai Fung

Din Tai Fung is a renowned Taiwanese chain restaurant founded in Taiwan in 1958. The restaurant specializes in soup dumplings and noodles. It recently opened its first and second locations in Seattle. The first location is next to the University of Washington, and second location is in Downtown Seattle. The restaurant was selected as one of the top ten restaurants in the world by The New York Times. It also has branches in other countries, such as Australia, Hong Kong, Thailand, UAE, Japan, Singapore, and South Korea.
